Degrees of Deception

They said the future was written in code,
A golden road where fortunes flowed.
STEM was the key, the promised land,
But the lock was rigged, the deck pre-planned.

Flooded the gates with dreams so bright,
Told us there’d be an endless fight,
For talent, for minds, for what we’d bring,
But the truth was hidden: a puppet’s string.

They built the glut to make us fall,
Cheap labor, endless, at their call.
Degrees in hand, we walk the line,
But the feast was theirs, and the scraps were mine.

Degrees of deception, sold as gold,
A market flooded, the lie retold.
Promises made, but none were kept,
Just a field of dreams where the workers wept.
We’re left to rot, cast off, betrayed,
By the very path we thought would save.

An unsatiable demand, they cried,
But it was a ruse, a baited tide.
A harvest ripe, a crop to cull,
To pick the few, the rest to dull.

The wage they crushed beneath their feet,
With surplus minds to make it cheap.
A pipeline built to feed their greed,
While we decay, unmet in need.

The dream was hollow, a cunning ploy,
A million graduates to employ,
But only the chosen ever rise,
The rest discarded, modern lies.

The conspirators laugh behind closed doors,
Counting profits while we’re ignored.
Our dreams were bait, their profits soared,
While we’re left broken, dreams abhorred.

Seven years for a future denied,
An assembly line where hope has died.
The glut remains, their profits swell,
Welcome to the wage-slave hell.


The Altar of Moloch, The Superorganism
